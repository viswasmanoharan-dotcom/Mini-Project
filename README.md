# Amazon Movies and TV Shows
This project provides information and analysis of TV shows available on Amazon's streaming platform. It includes details such as show titles, genres, release years, ratings, cast information, and descriptions. The project can be used for data analysis, recommendation systems, trend identification, and content exploration.

Features

Browse TV show information
Search and filter by genre, year, or rating
Analyze viewing trends and content categories
Generate recommendations based on user preferences
Visualize show ratings and popularity
Dataset Fields
Title
Genre
Release Year
Rating
Number of Seasons
Cast
Director/Creator
Description

Project Objectives

Analyze TV Show Content
Examine the distribution of TV shows by genre, release year, rating, and country.
Identify the most common categories available on the platform.
Study Content Trends
Analyze how the number of TV shows has changed over time.
Identify periods of significant growth in content additions.
Evaluate Audience Preferences
Determine which genres and ratings are most popular.
Identify factors that may influence viewer engagement.
Analyze Sales or Revenue Data
Examine sales performance across different products, categories, regions, or time periods.
Identify top-performing products and sales channels.
Identify Business Insights
Discover patterns that can help improve content strategy and sales performance.
Compare content availability with customer demand and purchasing behavior.
Support Decision-Making
Provide data-driven recommendations for content acquisition, marketing, and sales strategies.
Help optimize resource allocation and customer targeting

Findings

1. Content Distribution
TV shows make up a smaller portion of the catalog compared to movies.
The platform offers content across multiple genres, with drama, comedy, and documentary categories often being among the most common.
2. Release Year Trends
Most titles were released in recent years, indicating continuous expansion of the streaming library.
Older classic shows are available but represent a smaller percentage of the catalog.
3. Genre Analysis
Drama is often one of the most dominant genres.
Comedy, action, thriller, and documentary content also have strong representation.
Family and children's content forms a significant segment of the catalog.
4. Geographic Diversity
Content originates from multiple countries, showing Amazon's focus on international audiences.
The catalog includes both regional and global productions.
5. Ratings Analysis
A large share of titles are targeted toward adult audiences.
Family-friendly and teen-oriented content is also well represented.
6. Growth of Streaming Content
The number of new TV shows added generally increases over time, reflecting investment in original and licensed content.
7. Seasonal Trends
Certain years may show spikes in content additions due to platform expansion and increased content production.
